"God Horus Protecting King Nectanebo II---360–343 B.C.",
"The pharaoh Nectanebo II often invoked a very close
connection–even a merging–between himself and the falcon god
of kingship Horus In fact Nectanebo II was the focus of a
cult in which he was referred to as Nectanebo-the-Falcon
which could indeed be what is represented by this striking
conjunction of a powerful falcon and the monarch This idea
seems to be supported by the fact that Horus is not named on
the base only Nectanebo and the god Osiris-Mnevis at whose
sanctuary in Heliopolis the statue may have been erected
The composition may be read as a rebus of Nectanebo's
Egyptian name Nakhthorheb nakht the sword held in the king's
left hand Hor the falcon and heb the festival sign in the
king's right hand The last sign probably refers to the city
of Hebyt which was the site of the great temple constructed
by Nectanebo II for Isis mother of Horus.",
