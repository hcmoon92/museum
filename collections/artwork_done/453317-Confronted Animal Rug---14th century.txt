"Confronted Animal Rug---14th century",
"With its highly geometricized design this wool rug differs
markedly from the refined courtly carpets and represents an
earlier tradition of weaving that was popular in Europe
where rugs like this are found in fourteenth- and fifteenth
century churches and paintings In fact the depiction of a
rug with the same design as this in an early fifteenth-
century Sienese painting allowed for the dating of this
example This is one of only three complete rugs of such an
early date and its design of large confronted animals each
with a smaller animal inside probably derives from
contemporary textiles
