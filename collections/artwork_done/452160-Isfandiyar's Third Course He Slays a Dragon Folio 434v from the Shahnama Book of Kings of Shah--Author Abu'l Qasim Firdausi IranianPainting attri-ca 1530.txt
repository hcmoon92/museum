"This dramatic image illustrates the third of seven
challenges or courses that Prince Isfandiyar underwent en
route to freeing his sisters from captivity in Turan
Learning that he would encounter a dragon on his perilous
path Isfandiyar ordered a horse-drawn cart with a box in
which he could hide and from which spears projected Here the
dragon has appeared and is sucking the horses into its maw
soon to be impaled on the spears and slashed by Isfandiyar’s
sword The tightly coiled dragon slithering through the rocks
and breathing fire lends drama and menace to the scene which
otherwise contains many elements associated with Turkmen
painting such as the writhing bare bushes and squat figures
competently rendered by Qasim ibn 'Ali an artist from Shiraz
who was active at the Safavid court in the second quarter of
the sixteenth century.",

이 작품은 샤흐나메의 이야기를 생생하게 보여주는 동시에

그림의 색감과 붓놀림은 매우 세련되었고 용의 역동적인

이 작품은 단순히 이야기의 한 장면을 묘사하는

중요한 단서를 제공합니다


또한 이스판디야르의 용감함과 지혜를 보여주는 동시에 위험과
