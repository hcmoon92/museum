"This type of small bowl-shaped vessel from the imperial kiln
of the mid-Ming dynasty has been cherished as the pinnacle
of fine Chinese porcelain The decoration was achieved
through a complicated process First the design was drawn in
cobalt blue on the unfired vessel After glazing and high-
temperature firing the images were filled in with overglaze
enamel pigments and then it was fired at a lower temperature
to bring out the vibrant colors Known as “chicken cups” for
their imagery these vessels convey a core Chinese value
nurturing the young is essential for the continuation of the
family line.",
