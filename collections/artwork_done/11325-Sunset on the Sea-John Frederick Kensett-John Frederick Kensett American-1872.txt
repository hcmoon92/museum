"The group of paintings called The Last Summer's Work left
behind in Kensett's summer studio at Darien Connecticut at
his death in December 1872 was the subject of great wonder
and fascination among the artist's admirers friends and
eulogists who gathered in tribute to him later that month at
the Century Club Their wonder was a factor partly of sheer
novelty for none of the works had been seen before and as a
body they were interpreted to be Kensett's ultimate
testament of his vision and sensibility To the assembled
however none of those pictures better represented the
absolute expression of the artist's distilling and
suggestive eye than this in which he eliminated any evidence
of landfall but in a way reminiscent of few artists but J M
W Turner introduced a radiant sun suspended above the open
ocean It is pure light and water a bridal of the sea and sky
averred one of the eulogists and asked Is it presumption in
a poor novice in art like me to say that this is a great
picture.",
