"This exquisite brooch is one of the few extant examples of
plique-à-jour jewelry made by the New York firm of Marcus
Co whose reputation at its prime rivaled that of Tiffany  Co
Herman Marcus 1828–99 a German–born and Dresden–trained
jeweler arrived in New York in 1850 and worked for a number
of prestigious firms before establishing Marcus  Co in 1892
Following his death the company continued under the
direction of his two sons George Elder Marcus and William
Marcus

The brooch is a superb example of Marcus  Co’s work
in plique-à-jour enameling in which the cells of color have
no backing allowing light to shine through the transparent
enamel thereby creating the effect of stained glassOne of
the only jewelry firms of its day to succeed at this
challenging technique Marcus  Co followed the lead of such
innovative French designers as René Lalique The sensitive
3-dimensional sculpting of the sweet pea blossoms and leaves
as well as the naturalistic coloring of the enamels reflects
the Art Nouveau aesthetic that prevailed at the turn of the
century Indeed close parallels can be drawn with the
brilliant naturalistic work of Louis Comfort Tiffany whose
oeuvre is so well represented in the Met’s collection.",
