attributed to the renowned literati artist Dong Qichang
1555–1636 with an inscription by Dong’s collaborator Chen
Jiru 1558–1639 It depicts a celebrated work by the fourth-
century Chinese poet Tao Yuanming about a fisherman who
comes upon a secluded utopian village Whereas the
inscription and signatures on the copy resemble those on the
original Shukuya’s vibrant hues and gentle strokes mark a
departure from Dong’s monochromatic rustic style This
suggests that Shukuya unknowingly replicated a later
painting complete with meticulously forged inscriptions The
work thus demonstrates not only Shukuya’s skill in copying
