"The ceiling covering this gallery is a testament to the
resilience and persistence of traditional Islamic design in
Andalusia after the Christian Reconquista This so-called
mudéjar style was especially prevalent in the Aragon and
Castile-León regions of Spain where many Christian churches
incorporate similar ceilings The ceiling is comprised of
thirty carved painted and gilded pinewood panels The ceiling
originally was designed for a smaller space it was later
expanded explaining its somewhat uneven geometric pattern In
addition to the Islamic-inspired star pattern a whimsical
frieze of gilded running animals flying birds and swirling
vine scrolls decorates the cornice.",
