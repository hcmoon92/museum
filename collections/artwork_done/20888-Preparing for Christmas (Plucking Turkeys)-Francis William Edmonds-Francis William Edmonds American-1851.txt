"Born in Hudson New York Edmonds is one of few known
nineteenth-century American painters who pursued dual
careers in art and business becoming an influential figure
in the interrelated spheres of banking politics and culture
in New York City A regular contributor to the National
Academy of Design’s annual exhibitions Edmonds made his
reputation with popular genre scenes such as this work
inspired by seventeenth-century Dutch painting Preparing for
Christmas represents a seasonal activity on a farm While two
White men pluck turkeys for the holiday feast—unperturbed by
the winter cold—a younger Black figure positioned between
them inattentively warms his hands


In the tumultuous
decade of the 1850s when the United States was debating the
expansion of slavery into new Western territories Edmonds
and other New York artists often painted works with
conspicuously placed African Americans Generally portrayed
as humorous subjects some were depicted with greater empathy
Edmonds while educated in Quaker schools with a presumably
abolitionist sensibility here seems to emphasize the Black
character’s lack of productivity when compared to the White
workers The painting was featured in the 1852 auction of the
American Art-Union a subscription-based organization that
