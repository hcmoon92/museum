"Head of a Hippopotamus---ca. 1390–1352 B.C.",
"This extraordinarily lifelike animal head was once part of a
hippopotamus statue about three feet in length Comparisons
with other sculptures from the period indicate that it was
created during the reign of Amenhotep III The seated statues
that the king dedicated to the goddess Sakhmet are well
known their feline heads display hollow sinewed cheeks and
knobby facial bones similar to those on the hippo This head
may have come from Amenhotep's mortuary temple on the west
bank of the Nile opposite modern Luxor This temple was
mostly dismantled in ancient times but the site is marked by
two colossal statues of the king known as the colossi of
Memnon

Excavators have found another even larger hippo
statue also of Egyptian alabaster at the site Together with
hundreds of other sculptures—many of them representing
deities in animal form—the hippos would have served in
rituals procuring godlike status for the king On the
underside of the animal's jaw is an ancient drill hole It
may have been made for a metal support the head is heavy or
for the insertion of the hook of a harpoon during a ritual
hippopotamus hunt Traces of red paint are preserved in
furrows at the sides of the mouth.",

이 시대의 다른 조각상들과 비교해 보면 아멘호테프

아멘호테프 3세는 여신 사크메트에게 헌정한 앉아있는 조각상들로

이 하마 머리는 현대 룩소르 맞은편 나일

사원에서 온 것일 수 있습니다


이 사원은 고대에 대부분 해체되었지만 멤논의 거상으로

발굴자들은 이곳에서 이집트 알라바스터로 만들어진 더 크고

사용되었을 것입니다


하마 턱 아랫면에는 고대 드릴 구멍이 있습니다


이는 무거운 머리를 지탱하기 위한 금속 지지대를
위한 것이거나 의식적인 하마 사냥 중에 작살

이 작은 하마 머리는 단순한 조각상이 아니라

그 생생한 표현력과 세부 묘사는 제작자의 뛰어난
