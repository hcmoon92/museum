model in a wicker chair and relying on studies he had made
on the Normandy coast to furnish the beach scene behind her
Stylistically it reflects the impact of Renoir’s trip to
Italy in 1881–82 which inspired him to unite the “grandeur
and simplicity” he admired in Renaissance art with the
luminosity of Impressionism His new approach which he called
his “dry” manner is evident in the sitter’s face with its
carefully drawn features and smooth handling of paint The
medley of quick strokes in the background however displays
