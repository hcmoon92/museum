Manet’s stepson Léon Koëlla-Leenhoff recalled that he had
posed for this picture in 1861 when he was about ten years
old Manet dressed him in a seventeenth-century costume
adding a period sword as a prop—a tribute to the great
Spanish painters he admired notably Velázquez Critics
reviewed the work favorably on the five occasions that Manet
exhibited it between 1862 and 1872

In 1889 the New York
collector Erwin Davis donated Boy with a Sword and Young
Lady in 1866 89213 to the Metropolitan they were the first
works by Manet to enter a museum collection",
