"Gerard de Lairesse 1641–1711 was in his day a well-known
painter etcher and art theorist He suffered from congenital
syphilis which caused him to go blind about 1690 he
subsequently focused his energies on art theory By the time
this portrait was painted in 1665 the ravaging effects of
the disease were visible in his swollen features and bulbous
nose Recording his unfortunate appearance with an
uncompromising directness Rembrandt invested his subject
with an air of quiet dignity Although the sitter's theories
on the ideal in painting were antithetical to Rembrandt's
style which Lairesse disparagingly likened to liquid mud on
