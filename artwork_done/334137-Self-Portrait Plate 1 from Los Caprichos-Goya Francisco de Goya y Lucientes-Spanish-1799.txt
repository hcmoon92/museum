"In the first plate from the Caprichos Goya presents himself
as a sardonic observer of contemporary society even
nineteenth-century commentaries describe his censorious
somewhat pained expression as satirical The image has been
said to embody observation as a model of artistic endeavor
in contrast with imagination the subject of a second self-
portrait in the Caprichos hanging nearby After transferring
the preparatory red chalk drawing to the copperplate
1972118295 Goya made various corrections before applying
aquatint in two layers to achieve the tonal values of a wash
drawing Goya was one of the first Spanish artists to use the
aquatint process which produces tiny etched channels that
when inked print as a veil of tone
Los Caprichos was
published in Madrid in 1799 The series deal broadly with
superstition and human folly They were the artist’s personal
declaration that the chains of social backwardness had to be
broken if humanity was to advance attesting to his political
liberalism and his contempt for ignorance and intellectual
oppression Goya’s expression and elegant attire provide a
compelling introduction to the series announcing him as the
creator while revealing the gravity of his personality and
