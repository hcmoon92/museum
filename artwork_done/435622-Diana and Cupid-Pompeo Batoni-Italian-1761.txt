"Sir Humphrey Morice 1723–1785 son of a wealthy merchant and
director of the Bank of England commissioned this image of
the goddess of hunting It hung as a pendant or companion
piece to Batoni’s portrait of Morice re-clining in the Roman
countryside next to his dogs rifle and dead game Together
the works contrast modern and ancient Rome in the manner of
Giovanni Paolo Panini’s two canvases found nearby Diana’s
distinctive pose is based on a famous ancient statue of the
sleeping Cretan princess Ariadne Batoni inserts a literal
tension into this otherwise staid composition with the
elegantly lifted bow which Diana withholds from Cupid",
